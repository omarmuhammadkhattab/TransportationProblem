Transportation Problem

Pick a method below, step through the initial allocation, then follow the MODI improvement to the optimal answer.

Methods & MODI improvement
What is the transportation problem?
Minimize total shipping cost from m sources (supply Sᵢ) to n destinations (demand Dⱼ). Decision variables Xᵢⱼ = units shipped on route i→j at cost Cᵢⱼ. Objective: Minimize TC = ΣΣ Cᵢⱼ·Xᵢⱼ subject to all supply and demand constraints and Xᵢⱼ ≥ 0.
Balance & basic variables
Total Supply must equal Total Demand — add a dummy row or column with zero cost if not. A valid solution needs exactly m+n−1 allocated cells. Fewer allocations = degenerate (insert a zero-value allocation to compensate).
Phase 1 — Initial solution (choose a method)
NW Corner: fill from top-left, ignores costs — gives the worst initial result.
Least Cost: always fill the cheapest available cell — better result.
Vogel's: use opportunity-cost penalties to guide allocations — usually best.
Phase 2 — MODI / UV improvement (applied to whichever method you chose)
1. Set U₁=0. For each allocated cell, solve Cᵢⱼ=Uᵢ+Vⱼ to find all U and V values.
2. For each empty cell compute Zᵢⱼ=Uᵢ+Vⱼ−Cᵢⱼ. If all Z≤0 → current solution is optimal.
3. Otherwise: enter the cell with the most positive Z, trace a closed loop (+→−→+→...) through allocated cells, transfer the minimum (−) quantity. Repeat.

All three methods converge to the same optimal answer — a better initial solution just needs fewer MODI iterations.

Key takeaway
All three methods reach the same optimal TC = 435. The difference is how many MODI iterations are needed to get there — NW Corner requires 2, while Least Cost and Vogel's require only 1. The optimal routes are: E→B(5), E→D(10), F→B(10), F→C(15), G→A(5), G→D(5).
