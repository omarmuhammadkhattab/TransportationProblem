The transportation problem
Minimize the total shipping cost from m sources (supply) to n destinations (demand). Each route (i→j) carries Xᵢⱼ units at cost Cᵢⱼ per unit. Objective: Minimize TC = Σ Σ Cᵢⱼ·Xᵢⱼ.
Balance & basic variables
Total Supply must equal Total Demand (add a dummy row or column if not). A valid solution needs exactly m+n−1 allocated cells. Fewer = degenerate (insert a zero-value allocation).
The three methods
NW Corner — fill from top-left, ignores costs, worst result.
Least Cost — always pick the cheapest available cell, better result.
Vogel's — use opportunity-cost penalties, usually best initial result.
Start top-left. Allocate min(supply, demand). Cross out exhausted row or column. Move to adjacent cell.
Scan entire table for the lowest cost. Allocate maximum there, eliminate that row or column, repeat.
Compute penalty = (2nd min − min) per row and column. Allocate in the cheapest cell of the highest-penalty row/column. Recalculate and repeat.
